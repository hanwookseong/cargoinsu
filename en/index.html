FAQ

cargoinsu answers

The four questions shippers ask most about using an insurance broker.

Q. What is an insurance broker?

Under Article 89 of the Insurance Business Act, a broker is a licensed professional who compares and intermediates insurers on the client's behalf. The key difference: an agent is on the insurer's side; a broker is on yours. N2N Insurance Brokerage, which operates cargoinsu.com, is FSS-registered (Reg. No. 2026-012201).

Q. Who pays the fee?

The insurer pays our brokerage fee. Nothing is added to the premium you pay. This follows Article 98 of the Insurance Business Act (prohibition of special benefits) and is what lets us compare 6 insurers for you at no extra cost.

Q. Why compare 6 insurers?

Underwriting appetite and rates differ by insurer depending on cargo type, route and loss history. A single quote can miss your best terms. cargoinsu reviews AIG · Chubb · DB · Hyundai Marine & Fire · KB · Meritz together (alphabetical order).

Q. What happens after I request a quote?

① Review of your details (1 business day) → ② underwriting check across 6 insurers → ③ results returned (1–2 business days) → ④ you compare and choose → ⑤ application & certificate issue. Marine cargo can also be quoted in 30 seconds via the calculator above.

※ Our brokerage fee is paid by the insurer and is not added to your premium (Article 98, Insurance Business Act).
