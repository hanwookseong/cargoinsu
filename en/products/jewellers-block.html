1. Overview — a global standard wording

Jeweller's Block insurance covers high-value goods — precious metals, gemstones, fine watches, luxury goods (handbags, accessories), art, antiques and rare coins — on an All Risks basis across stock, transit, exhibition and trade shows, under a worldwide standard wording. It suits jewellery/watch dealers, duty-free stores, auction houses, museums, galleries, luxury-goods distributors and private collectors.

2. Key features

  • 01
    All Risks Worldwide

    Worldwide cover across store, vault, transit, exhibition, working, repair and auction

  • 02
    Memo & Consignment

    Loss/theft while on memo (approval) between client and dealer — a core industry endorsement

  • 03
    Mysterious Disappearance

    Unexplained-loss cover within a limit (typically 10–20% of stock)

  • 04
    Exhibition Extension

    Automatic cover for temporary moves to overseas fairs, exhibitions and auctions

3. Main losses covered

  • Fire, theft or robbery in store/vault storage
  • Loss on exhibit (shows, display cases)
  • Loss in transit (carriage, recipient transport)
  • Loss/theft while on client memo (approval)
  • Damage during working or repair
  • Postal/courier transit loss or theft (endorsement)
  • Transit cover during auction/appraisal periods

Main exclusions

  • Wilful acts, insider dishonesty (separate Crime/Fidelity)
  • Improper storage (vault unused, left unlocked)
  • Natural wear, corrosion, discoloration (poor condition)
  • War, terrorism, confiscation (excluded from endorsement)
  • High-value items not individually appraised (over limit)
  • Genuine/fake disputes (appraisal disputes handled separately)

5. Example scenarios (illustrative)

Scenario A

Jewellery dealer store (Cheongdam / Myeongdong)

Stock appraised at around ₩5bn (diamonds, fine watches): ₩3bn vault / ₩1bn display / ₩1bn in transit. ALL RISKS Worldwide + Mysterious Disappearance recommended. The premium is confirmed after the insurer's underwriting review of appraised value, vault grade, guarding spec and loss history.

Scenario B

Auction house (one-off sale, ₩10bn)

A one-off auction of contemporary art and antique watches. Cover from appraisal to sale to delivery (1 month), on a short-period pro-rata rate. The premium is confirmed after the insurer reviews lot value and the exhibition/auction schedule.

Scenario C

Museum permanent display (public/private)

Around ₩20bn on display (paintings, sculpture, installation). All-Risks permanent + loan/touring endorsement. The premium is set after a combined review of appraised values, display environment and security grade.

Things to note

  • When applying, confirm the basics of the contract — product name, period of insurance, premium payment term and the insured — and be sure to receive and read the policy wording and product brochure.
  • Coverage exclusions are set out in each insurer's policy wording and brochure; refer to them for details, as claims may be limited by exclusions or payment-limitation grounds.
  • If the insured event has already occurred when the contract is concluded, the contract is void.
  • Duty of disclosure: the policyholder, the insured or their agent must answer the application/questionnaire truthfully; otherwise a claim may be declined or the contract cancelled.
  • Cooling-off: a policyholder may generally withdraw the application within 15 days of receiving the certificate; note that commercial (corporate) insurance taken out by a professional financial consumer cannot be withdrawn.
  • Providing special benefits in connection with an insurance contract is punishable under the Insurance Business Act.
